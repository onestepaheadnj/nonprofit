High Cholesterol

Synopsis

Presentation

Game

Cholesterol is a type of fat found in your blood which is necessary to make cells, make hormones for growth, digest food and much more. We gain cholesterol from our food, and cholesterol floats around in our blood. Cholesterol does not move around through the body on its own: it combines with proteins to travel through the bloodstream. However, if we intake too much cholesterol, it can stick in the walls of blood vessels, causing them to become narrower and possibly clogged, leading to a heart attack. As such, high cholesterol can be a dangerous disease and can put you at great risk. The key to prevention is through a healthy lifestyle, which includes eating healthy food like fruits and vegetables and staying active.

We started this event with an interactive presentation, first describing the existence of. and the differences between, HDL and LDL cholesterol. We provided various examples of sources of HDL, or “good” cholesterol, and LDL, or “bad” cholesterol. We delved into the symptoms and complications that someone with high cholesterol could face in their life, including heart disease. Ending the presentation by explaining methods to prevent high cholesterol, we opened the room to questions.

The game for this event was called Artery Attack. The children were separated into four teams, and each team represented their own colored artery. Each of the four arteries was clogged with 10 orange balls, representing bad cholesterol. In order to clear their arteries, children had to run into a designated area and bring back printed food cards that they thought helped reduce/prevent high cholesterol. If they were correct, a ball was removed, with a ball being added otherwise. Our members gave fun facts and additional information every time the children brought back food in order to educate the children on their choices and the winning team, the team who cleared all their cholesterol first, was given a prize.
