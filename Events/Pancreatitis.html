Pancreatitis is a potentially life-threatening and contagious disease where the pancreas becomes inflamed. It can be caused by smoking, prescription drugs, alcoholism, family history, and cystic fibrosis. Symptoms include rapid pulse, abdominal pain, loss of appetite, nausea, vomiting, and sweating, and long-term complications include diabetes, breathing problems, malnutrition, and pancreatic cancer. The best way to prevent complications, as for all diseases, is to have a healthy diet and engage in regular exercise; abstaining from smoking has also proven to prevent the development of complications

The first activity of the event was a presentation on pancreatitis which highlighted the causes, symptoms, effects, and measures of prevention for the disease in kid-friendly terms; we left time for answering any of the childrensâ€™ questions before proceeding to our next activity.

We ended our event with a discussion of a model of the pancreas, which we created out of clay. We used the model to describe how the pancreas functions in the body and how it is specifically affected by pancreatitis. We also performed an experiment using milk, food coloring, and dish soap in order to illustrate the role of bile in the body.

Our game for this event was titled Pancreatitis Bingo. Using the information they learned from the presentation, the children answered questions related to pancreatitis on their bingo boards; the questions addressed topics such as affected body parts, symptoms, and causes. If they answered correctly, they were able to cross off the related symbol, and participants continued until they reached a traditional bingo. As usual, prizes were handed to those victorious.
