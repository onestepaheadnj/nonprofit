Pneumonia

Synopsis

Presentation

Experiment

Game

Pneumonia is when tiny sacs in your lungs get filled up with fluid.This makes it harder to breathe and can cause problems for many people, especially children and older people. This is caused by the filling of the small sacs; there are many reasons, from bacteria to fungi. Symptoms can include fever and phlegm to name a couple, and it can be prevented with easy steps like sleeping properly.

The event began with an introduction for the children with music and small talk before the start of the presentation on pneumonia. We explained a general description, causes, and symptoms of Alzehemeir’s disease and dementia, before moving into preventive measures and healthy lifestyle changes. We concluded with a Q&A session.

In small and supervised groups, we demonstrated what it is like to have pneumonia with lung-like contraptions using bottles and balloons to show the difficulty in breathing that can be present. A discussion took place connecting the model with the changes that take a place in our lungs during pneumonia.

Our interactive game used small cards to improve our understanding of pneumonia. We created lung models that were filled with “fluid strips” from pneumonia, and the players had to remove all the strips to win by answering our review questions correctly. We concluded with a review session talking about what everyone learned.
