Meningitis

Synopsis

Presentation

Experiment

Game

Meningitis is the inflammation of the meninges, which are the brain and spinal cord membranes. It can be a potentially life-threatening infection and is contagious. The condition is caused by infections, which include: bacterial infections, viral infections, and fungal infections. Symptoms include fever, photophobia (sensitivity to light), rashes, and cold hands and feet. Meningitis may lead to several long-term complications such as vision loss, arthritis, and kidney problems. One can prevent Meningitis by decreasing the risk of infection through regular exercise and a healthy diet.

The event began with an interactive presentation for the children on the disease in general; after highlighting the cause, symptoms, effects, and measures of prevention for meningitis, we proceeded to answer any question the children had left. Following the presentation, we had a Q&A session which clarified questions such as, “what organisms cause meningitis?” and “Is meningitis communicable?”

The event ended with an application of the information we learned, as we asked the kids to compare brain scans of patients with meningitis. They learned how the information translates to real-world diagnoses and understood the implications of meningitis far better than before.

With their newfound knowledge, the children then competed in Meningitis Bingo. Instead of your typical number spaces, each space that bingo board stood for a special symbol connected to meningitis; these symbols include body parts affected by the disease, organisms that cause the disease, and different exercises to prevent infection. To cross out a symbol and get that Bingo, children have to answer questions pertaining to a symbol correctly in order to cross out a symbol. Of course, we had to have prizes. We planned to hand out glow sticks at the end to only winners, but all the kids ended up leaving with a prize.
