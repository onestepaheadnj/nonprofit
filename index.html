One Step Ahead

At One Step Ahead, we really only have one goal :

to inform our children how to stay healthy.

Why Did We Launch One Step Ahead?

Diseases are heavily underdiscussed in our society. It’s simply a fact.

Take tooth decay. Everyone knows about cavities and how important going to the dentist is. Right? How many people know that tooth decay is the most prevalent childhood disease, according to the American Academy of Pediatric Dentistry (AAPD)? How many children know that even some healthy foods packed with carbohydrates, like bagels and granola bars, are potentially just as harmful for your teeth as sugary foods? How about this shocker: you miss cleaning 40% of your tooth surfaces if you don’t floss. Either most of us do not know or do not appreciate the importance of flossing, because according to a recent health survey, only 30% of the United States population flosses daily.

At One Step Ahead, we aspire to teach children about the importance of healthy by making them understand how to stay healthy. Ideas never stick unless they are understood. In our events, presnetaions, and games, we discuss how our human body works, the different functions of our organs and body parts, the best ways to stay healthy and active, and most importantly, diseases that are under-discussed in our soceity, like tooth decay.

The Three Pillars That Guide Our Events

Awareness

Our campaign revolves around letting people know. Be it through detailed presentations, answering curious questions, or passing our knowledge through simple conversation, we aim to give the children all the facts so they understand why and how to be healthy.

Prevention

The best way to deal with disease is to prevent ever being infected by diseases. By The time one becomes infected, the damage is already done. We always include a prevention component in our presentations and games, and we encourage children to stay healthy by exercising, having a balanced diet, and other related measures.

Engagement

We can provide as much information as we want, but if the children ultimately are disengaged, the information will go through one ear and out the other. We always try to keep the kids interested by encouraging questions, creating new and creative games ourselves for each and every event, and by simply getting to know the children we teach on a closer level.
